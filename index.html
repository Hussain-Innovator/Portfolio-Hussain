I'm a BS Software Engineering graduate from Iqra University with a strong foundation in artificial intelligence, computer vision, and deep learning. My final year project — Fine-Grained Classification of Fetal Ultrasound Planes — developed FetalNet, a custom MobileNetV2 + SE Block architecture trained on 12,400 ultrasound images, achieving a test accuracy of 93.01%.

I believe great engineering is as much about clarity of thinking as it is about writing code. I strive to build systems that are interpretable, maintainable, and genuinely impactful — especially in domains where the stakes are as high as healthcare.

Ablation Study: Contribution of SE Block, Convolutional Layers & Pooling Strategy in FetalNet
FYP Internal Research · Iqra University
Systematic component-level ablation study comparing the full FetalNet (93.01% accuracy) against three ablated variants: removing the SE Block (92.00%), removing the second convolutional layer (92.64%), and replacing MaxPool with AvgPool (92.37%). Results confirmed the positive contribution of each architectural component to final performance.

FYP Chapter 7 — Testing & Evaluation · Iqra University
Qualitative evaluation of four Explainable AI techniques applied to FetalNet predictions. 2026
Hybrid CNN — CIFAR-10 Image Classifier Technical Report
Independent Research · Self Study & Technical Report · Author: Hussain
Designed and implemented a custom hybrid CNN architecture for CIFAR-10 image classification by integrating Residual Connections, Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) Attention, and Depthwise Separable Convolutions. Trained on the CIFAR-10 dataset containing 60,000 labeled images across 10 object categories. Achieved 90.93% test accuracy, 98.71% Top-3 accuracy, and a Macro F1-score of 0.91 using MixUp augmentation, label smoothing, cosine annealing learning rate scheduling, and linear warmup. Converted the trained model to ONNX format and deployed it as interactive Streamlit and Hugging Face applications using ONNX Runtime for efficient CPU inference.
